A minimalist, on-device color palette tool for artists and illustrators. Capture a palette from any photo, generate harmonies from your own taste, export straight to Procreate or Adobe — all without ever leaving your browser.

Why no noob color

Built for illustrators, not engineers

Every other palette tool feels designed by software people. no noob color speaks the language of artists: gestural ink, hand-drawn UI, real WCAG numbers, ICC color profiles, Procreate-native export. No subscriptions, no telemetry, no backend — your taste lives on your device.

  • On-device All processing happens in your browser. Nothing uploaded, nothing stored on a server.
  • Open-source typography Inter + JetBrains Mono (SIL OFL), Tippy.js for tooltips (MIT). No proprietary dependencies.
  • Honest math k-means in L*a*b* perceptual space, mode-snapped to actual pixels. WCAG contrast on every swatch.
  • Multi-format export Procreate .swatches, Adobe .ase, plain text — all generated client-side. Hand-rolled ZIP, no libraries.

How it works

From photo to palette

Drop any image and five dominant colors are extracted using k-means clustering in perceptual L*a*b* color space — the math behind how human eyes actually group color, not raw RGB distance.

  • Honest swatches Each swatch snaps to an actual pixel from your photo, never a muddy averaged centroid. The colors are real, not synthesized.
  • WCAG inline Every swatch shows its contrast ratio against black/white with AA · AAA · FAIL badges right on the strip.
  • Color profile Switch output between sRGB, Display P3, Adobe RGB or CMYK to match the medium you're designing for.
  • Export anywhere Procreate .swatches, Adobe .ase, or copy as text. Generated client-side, no server, no telemetry.

How it works

Trained on your taste

You pick 30 colors you love from a slow auto-scrolling carousel — those become your taste profile, saved locally. Every palette generated here is drawn from your own colors. Color theory just decides which 5.

  • Harmonies Auto, Monochromatic, Analogous, Complementary, Triadic, Split-Complementary or Tetradic. Hover any option for what it does.
  • Temperature Filter your taste pool by warm hues (red · orange · yellow), cool (blue · green · violet), or any.
  • Keyword bias Type a feeling — energy, calm, gold, sunset, neon, lüks, gece, deniz — 100+ EN/TR keywords map to hue/sat/lit ranges.
  • WCAG enforcement Pick a minimum contrast (3:1 / 4.5:1 / 7:1). Adjacent swatches always meet your threshold.
  • Re-trainable Hit "Retrain my taste" anytime. The 30 colors define everything — change them, change every output.
